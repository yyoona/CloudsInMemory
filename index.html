Clouds in Memory

Yoona Yang
Information Desgin Studio
Aalto University
2023

Humans communicate sensibly with the surrounding entities and remember moments and situations.
Then, how do we remember the appearance of the sky above our heads, a place our gaze often overlooks, in a sensual way?

In this project, the clouds and the optical phenomena are observed during 6 days in different spots of the campus, Aalto University, Finland.

6 days clouds observation

I observed clouds by taking photos with a mobile phone. The archived photos of clouds are analysed quantitatively through the mobile app, "Cloud-a-Day", having a function of AI-based cloud classifier.

The most seen cloud type

The AI cloud classifier sometimes does not recognize a specific cloud as a particular type of cloud since the photo includes different clouds in itself or the clouds of the photo are somewhat obscure. Thus, I may have variable clouds in my memory according to the result of the AI cloud classifier. This uncertain phenomenon reminds me that clouds themselves are also movable and changeable.

How far I was from clouds

I stopped at various location in different time during the observing days. To look into the relation between myself and clouds, I try to check how far I was from clouds with GPS Altitude data.

Time & Altitude

The average time I took the photo of a cloud was 09:29:53 on May 25, 2023 (1369031411 GPS time).

Way of observing

I took a photograph as a way of observing clouds. The act of capturing the shape of clouds through photos is simple but it involves various motility, spatiality, and subjectivity in sensual communication with clouds.

Because I intentionally or unintentionally made some body movement before shooting the photo. I set the zoom to 1-5 times and sometimes tilt the phone by more degrees to capture the momentary clouds with a smartphone camera. These body movements vary the metadata of the photos, for instance, Zoom and Acceleration data.

Zoom & Acceleration

The most dominant zoom level from x1 to x5 is "x3"
Also, the average accelerationZ value is 0.4822869106.

Clouds generated by memory

So far, I've analyzed the clouds in my memory based on the AI tool and statistics to find out which clouds account for the most dominant proportion in my memory. Further, I looked into the way of observing based on the time, location data and photographic-related data, such as the zoom and the acceleration value.

Now, I wonder what the overall appearance of the clouds in my mind during the six days looks like. What if I use another AI tool to generate the invisible memory of the cloud based on the archived cloud photos? Would be the memory about the clouds encouraged to be more fluently vivid?
